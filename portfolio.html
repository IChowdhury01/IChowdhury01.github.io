itsMe: Attachable Smart Lock

A new, revolutionary IoT smart lock designed for low-cost, modular assembly and seamless installation with no required home renovation. The lock can be 3D-printed and assembled at under $60, freely attaches onto deadbolt locks, and is controlled remotely via Bluetooth with the itsMe Android app. itsMe's design is fully open-source, and can be customized to fit locks of any size. 3D-printable CAD models for assembly are available on the project's GitHub page.

NYC Restaurant Mapper

A web app that searches for up to 5 different food preferences or restaurants simultaneously, then displays the best matches on a map of NYC. You may then visit any particular restaurant's Yelp profile by clicking its map marker.

  • Learn more
  • Demo

Movie Review Sentiment Analyzer

App that trains a recurrent neural network for the sentiment analysis of movie reviews, and allows the user to input his own reviews for binary sentiment prediction. The model was built using TensorFlow, Keras, and the IMDB dataset of 50,000 highly polar movie reviews, and recorded an accuracy of 93.54% during testing.

  • Learn more

MATCH: Friend-Matching Platform

Web app that allows users to find friends with similar interests in their area. MATCH's friend-matching algorithm utilizes user geolocation data, hobbies, and other personal preferences to optimize results. This app also features a chat system, account management, PBKDF2 encryption, and cookie support.

  • Learn more

Speed Racer

A pure hardware implementation of the popular 2D arcade game, Speed Racer. There are three score displays: player score, death score, and level. Players are scored by how long they survive. At higher levels, the speed of obstacles increases. The game ends when the player dies three times.

  • Learn more
  • Demo

Coffee Can Radar

A fully functioning radar built out of two large coffee cans. The radar detects objects in front of it based on their range and velocity, and displays monitored signals to a connected computer. Detected objects are indicated by dark blue or red spikes, depending on their range and velocity.

  • Learn more
  • Demo

Project Kutemba: Low-Cost Prosthesis

A project to develop low-cost, mass-producible prosthetic limbs for use by patients in rural Soroti, Uganda who suffer from malnutrition diabetes. We worked with experts from the New York Hospital for Special Surgery and Albert Einstein College of Medicine to design, manufacture, and stress test a prototype prosthesis.
